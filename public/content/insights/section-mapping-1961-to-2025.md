The Income-tax Act, 2025 keeps the substance of the old law but renumbers almost everything. If you have Section 80C, Section 139, or Section 194J committed to memory, you will need new coordinates from Tax Year 2026-27 onwards. This is a bookmarkable quick-reference for the provisions we field the most questions about. It pairs with our interactive mapper tool for anything not listed here.

:::summary

### Key Takeaways

- The 2025 Act keeps the substance of the old law but renumbers almost every section from Tax Year 2026-27.
- High-frequency moves: 80C → 123, 80D → 126, 139 → 263, 44AD/ADA/AE → 58, 45 → 67, 192 → 392, 206C → 394.
- For FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27) returns filed in 2026, you still use the old 1961 section numbers.
- Always verify a citation against the official gazette or the department’s mapper before quoting it in a filing.
  :::

> **Read this first.** For **FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)** returns — the ones filed in 2026 — you still use the **old (1961) section numbers**. The new numbers apply only from **Tax Year 2026-27** returns. Always confirm a citation against the official gazette or the Income Tax Department's mapping utility before quoting it in a filing, notice reply, or agreement.

## How to use this

- **Client meetings and drafting.** Keep this table handy so you can translate on the fly. Where a document (a partnership deed, a service agreement, a standard notice reply) hard-codes an old section number, update it to the new reference for Tax Year 2026-27 matters.
- **TDS software.** The new Act replaces standalone section numbers on challans and returns with a code-driven system under Section 393. Do not rely on the code numbers you see quoted informally online — they vary between write-ups. Pull the exact codes from your updated, CBDT-aligned TDS software or the official notification.
- **When in doubt, verify.** This sheet covers the high-frequency provisions only. For the full 500-plus section map, use our mapper tool or the Income Tax Department's official utility.
