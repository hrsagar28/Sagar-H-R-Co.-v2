In 2025, several important GST compliance changes will be implemented. Businesses must be prepared to adapt to these new requirements to avoid penalties and ensure smooth operations.

:::summary
### Key Takeaways
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) will be mandatory for all GST taxpayers from April 1, 2025.
- E-way bills can only be generated for documents not older than 180 days.
- Businesses with AATO of ₹10 crores or more must report e-invoices within 30 days.
- Mandatory ISD registration for companies with branches in multiple states from April 1, 2025.
:::

## Mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

To bolster the security of the GST portal, the government is introducing MFA in a phased manner:
- **From January 1, 2025:** Mandatory for taxpayers with an Annual Aggregate Turnover (AATO) exceeding ₹200 million.
- **From February 1, 2025:** Applicable to taxpayers with an AATO exceeding ₹50 million.
- **From April 1, 2025:** Enforced for all taxpayers, regardless of their turnover.

## New Rules for E-Way Bills (EWB) and E-invoicing

Several changes are being made to curb fraudulent practices and ensure timely movement of goods:
- **Restriction on Old Documents:** Effective January 1, 2025, e-way bills will be restricted to base documents not older than 180 days.
- **Limit on EWB Extensions:** The total extension period for e-way bills will be capped at 360 days from the original generation date.
- **E-invoicing Time Limit:** From April 1, 2025, businesses with an AATO of ₹10 crores or more must report their e-invoices within 30 days of the invoice date.

## Mandatory ISD Registration

Starting April 1, 2025, companies with branches in multiple states must register as an Input Service Distributor (ISD) to ensure the efficient distribution of Input Tax Credit (ITC). This is a significant change that will require affected businesses to re-evaluate their compliance processes.
